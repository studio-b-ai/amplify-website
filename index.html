02 — The Moat
Five tools. One approval. No audit trail.

Most operators run PhantomBuster for signals, a separate scoring spreadsheet, another tool for drafting, a third for publishing, and a Slack DM to “approve.” Every handoff leaks context. Every manual step breaks the chain. Amplify closes the loop.

  1. You’re getting signals. You don’t know which ones matter.
  2. Someone’s drafting manually. They’re guessing your voice.
  3. Approvals happen in DMs. There’s no record.
  4. Publish happens whenever. There’s no timestamp.
  5. The ledger is a spreadsheet. Your compliance team hates it.
  • L1 + L3 solve #1
    ICP scoring runs every signal through your defined criteria. Below the cutoff, it doesn’t reach you.
  • L3 solves #2
    Claude calibrates against your approved drafts. Your voice, not a template.
  • L3 + ledger solve #3 + #5
    Every Slack approval is a ledger entry: approver, timestamp, channel, response time. Exportable CSV + JSON.
  • L4 solves #4
    Publish triggers only on approval. Timestamp and LinkedIn IDs write to the ledger on execution.
